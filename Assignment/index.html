Treatment Overview

Heart bypass surgery, also known as coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG), is a common and highly effective medical procedure for treating coronary artery disease. This condition occurs when the blood vessels that supply the heart with oxygen and nutrients become narrow or blocked, often due to the buildup of cholesterol and other substances.
During a heart bypass procedure, a surgeon creates new pathways for blood flow by using a healthy blood vessel from another part of the body or a synthetic graft. This "bypass" allows blood to reach the heart muscle, bypassing the blocked or narrowed arteries.

Eligibility For Treatment

Eligibility for heart bypass surgery, also known as coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG), is determined by a comprehensive assessment by a healthcare team. Typically, candidates for this procedure have severe coronary artery disease (CAD) that significantly restricts blood flow to the heart muscle, leading to chest pain (angina) or increasing the risk of a heart attack.


Candidates should have extensive blockages in the coronary arteries that cannot be managed with medications or less invasive procedures. Symptoms: Presence of symptoms like chest pain, shortness of breath, or fatigue despite medical therapy.


Heart bypass surgery is a significant procedure, and eligibility is determined on a case-by-case basis. Patients are encouraged to consult with a healthcare professional to assess their specific situation and determine the most appropriate treatment plan.

Preparation Before Treatment

Before undergoing heart bypass treatment, thorough preparation is vital. T his includes consultations with your medical team to discuss the procedure, potential risks, and expected outcomes. Your doctor may recommend lifestyle modifications like diet and exercise to optimize your health. Preoperative tests, including blood work and imaging, will be conducted to ensure you're fit for surgery. It's crucial to provide a complete medical history and inform your healthcare team about all medications and allergies. Additionally, making necessary arrangements for post-surgery recovery and having a support system in place is essential. Adequate preparation ensures a smoother and safer heart bypass procedure.

About Treatment

Heart bypass surgery, medically known as coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG), is a life-saving procedure used to treat coronary artery disease (CAD). CAD occurs when the blood vessels that supply the heart muscle with oxygen and nutrients become narrow or blocked, often due to the buildup of cholesterol and plaque. During a heart bypass surgery, a cardiothoracic surgeon creates new pathways for blood to flow to the heart muscle by using grafts, which are typically segments of healthy blood vessels from other parts of the body, such as the leg or chest. These grafts bypass the obstructed or narrowed coronary arteries, restoring proper blood flow to the heart. The surgery is performed under general anesthesia, and the patient's heart is temporarily stopped while a heart-lung machine takes over its function. The surgeon then carefully attaches the grafts, rerouting blood around the blocked arteries.

Post Treatment Care Treatment

Post-treatment care is a crucial phase in the healing journey, focusing on recovery and overall well-being. It involves monitoring, medication management, and lifestyle adjustments to ensure the treatment's success. Patients receive guidance on managing potential side effects, maintaining a healthy diet, and adhering to follow-up appointments. Emotional support and mental health resources are often integral, helping patients transition smoothly into their daily lives. This phase empowers individuals to regain their strength and resume their routines, promoting a full recovery and a renewed sense of vitality and confidence. Post-treatment care is a holistic approach to nurturing physical and emotional health after medical interventions.
